    But what do we mean by learning? Mitchell (1997) provides a succinct deﬁnition:

    “A computer program is said to learn from experienceE with respect to someclass of tasksT and performance measure P, if its performance at tasks in T, as measured by P, improves with experience E.” One can imagine a wide variety ofexperiences E, tasks T, and performance measures P, and we do not attempt in this book to formally deﬁne what may be used for each of these entities. Instead,in the following sections, we provide intuitive descriptions and examples of the diﬀerent kinds of tasks, performance measures, and experiences that can be used to construct machine learning algorithms.
